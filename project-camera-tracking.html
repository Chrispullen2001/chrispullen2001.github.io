Camera Tracking System

Senior Design / Capstone Project

Python ROS2 OpenCV Belt-Driven Gantry Controls

Overview

Developed as a senior capstone project, this system is a closed-loop automated camera tracking platform designed to capture continuous high-speed motion data across extended ranges. The system combines mechanical design, motor sizing and selection, embedded controls, and computer vision to track moving subjects with precision — without requiring manual camera operation.

The platform uses a belt-driven linear gantry to move the camera along a track, with ROS2 handling inter-process communication and OpenCV providing real-time vision feedback to drive the closed-loop tracking controller.

Technical Details

Mechanical Design

The gantry uses a belt-and-pulley drive system to translate the camera carriage along a linear track. The structure was designed for rigidity and minimal backlash to support accurate positioning across the full travel range.

Motor Sizing & Control

Motors were sized based on load, acceleration requirements, and target velocity. The control loop commands motor velocity in real time to keep the tracked subject centered in the camera frame.

Computer Vision (OpenCV)

OpenCV processes the live camera feed to detect and localize the subject within the frame. The pixel offset from center is used as the error signal fed into the tracking controller.

ROS2 Architecture

ROS2 nodes handle vision processing, control computation, and motor command publishing, providing a modular, testable software architecture that separates concerns cleanly across the pipeline.

Key Challenges

  • Achieving low-latency vision-to-motor response for accurate high-speed subject tracking
  • Mechanically designing a rigid, low-backlash belt drive system within project constraints
  • Tuning the closed-loop controller to avoid oscillation while maintaining tracking responsiveness
  • Integrating mechanical, electrical, and software subsystems developed in parallel into a cohesive system

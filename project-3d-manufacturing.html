Production Engineering

Custom 3D Manufacturing for Publicis / P&G

FDM 3D Printing CAD SolidWorks Post-Processing Production Engineering

Overview

Designed and delivered a mascot-scale 3D-printed chain and pendant for a commercial client (Publicis / P&G) under an accelerated sub-one-week deadline. The project demanded rapid CAD iteration, strategic print planning, and coordinated post-processing to meet strict visual, structural, and scheduling requirements — all at large production scale.

This was a production engineering challenge as much as a design one: the deliverable required maintaining consistent quality across many printed parts while meeting a tight deadline that left no room for significant rework.

Technical Details

CAD Design

The chain and pendant were modeled in CAD with careful attention to link geometry, tolerances for post-processing, and the structural requirements of a large-scale prop that would be handled and displayed.

Print Strategy

Print orientation, layer height, infill, and support strategy were optimized to balance surface quality, print time, and part strength — critical when running many prints in parallel against a hard deadline.

Post-Processing

Parts underwent sanding, priming, and finishing to achieve the client's visual standards. Post-processing was coordinated across the production run to maintain consistency at scale.

Schedule Management

With less than a week from brief to delivery, print queues, material availability, and finishing steps were managed in parallel to ensure on-time delivery without sacrificing quality.

Key Challenges

  • Designing chain link geometry that printed cleanly and assembled correctly at mascot scale
  • Optimizing print settings to maximize throughput without compromising visual quality
  • Coordinating parallel print runs and post-processing stages under a sub-one-week deadline
  • Meeting commercial client quality expectations with FDM manufacturing constraints

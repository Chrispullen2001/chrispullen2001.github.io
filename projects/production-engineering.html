Production Engineering: Custom 3D Manufacturing (Publicis / P&G)

Back to Projects

Project Overview

This project demonstrates rapid-turnaround custom manufacturing for a high-profile commercial client. Under an accelerated sub-one-week deadline, a mascot-scale 3D-printed chain and pendant were designed, fabricated, and delivered for a Publicis/P&G campaign.

The project showcases the full production engineering workflow: rapid CAD iteration incorporating client feedback, print strategy optimization for visual quality and structural integrity, and coordinated post-processing to meet exacting aesthetic and functional requirements at scale—all while maintaining production quality and deadline adherence.

Key Achievement: Successfully delivered production-grade deliverable under aggressive sub-one-week timeline while managing rapid design iteration, complex post-processing, and full client satisfaction on first delivery.

Production Insights

Rapid CAD Iteration Strategy

Leveraged parametric CAD design principles to enable fast modification cycles. Key features were designed as variables, allowing dimensional or geometric changes to propagate automatically without full model reconstruction.

Print Time Optimization

Analyzed printer capabilities and material constraints to develop optimal layer height, infill percentage, and support structure strategies, reducing total print time by 30% without compromising final quality.

Quality Control Checkpoints

Established staged quality verification throughout production: CAD dimensional checks, post-print dimensional verification, and final assembly inspection—ensuring issues detected early could be resolved within timeline.
